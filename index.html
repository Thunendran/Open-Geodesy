A Generalized Algorithm for the Gravitational Potential of a Homogeneous Cuboid

Authors: Thunendran Periyandy and Michael Bevis
Year: 2025
Field: Physical Geodesy, Gravity Field, Potential Theory, Numerical Methods
DOI: 10.3844/pisp.2025.7.17

Gravity Physical Geodesy Potential Theory Cuboid Numerical Methods

Research Reflection

This paper presents a generalized algorithm for computing the gravitational potential of a homogeneous cuboid. Its central contribution is that the formulation works inside, on, and outside the cuboid, removing an important spatial restriction found in many earlier potential-field algorithms.

Important Points

  • Develops an analytical formulation for the gravitational potential of a uniform-density cuboid.
  • Uses a hexagonal graph structure to organize logarithmic and arctangent terms.
  • Handles numerical singularities to improve stability and correct sign allocation.
  • Provides pseudocode and implementations in Python, Matlab, and Julia.
  • Introduces the Laplacian test as a validation method for the algorithm and codes.

Impact

The work is important for physical geodesy, gravity-field modelling, and computational potential theory. It provides a stable way to compute the gravitational potential of cuboidal mass elements without restricting the evaluation point to the exterior of the body.

Direction of Thought

Direction Type

Changes direction / Opens new direction

Simple Explanation

This paper shifts the discussion from exterior-only gravitational potential computation toward a spatially general formulation that can be used inside, on, and outside a cuboid.

Author Perspective

As the first author, my intention is to make gravitational potential computation for cuboidal bodies more general, stable, and easier to implement for geodetic and geophysical applications.
